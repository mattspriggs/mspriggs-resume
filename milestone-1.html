Resume Website for Matt Spriggs

Purpose, Audience and Goal

Purpose

This website will assist Matt in making a successful career change from other industries to the Information Technology field, specifically Fullstack Web Development after completing the bootcamp and moving to New Zealand

Audience

There are two primary target audiences for this website (NOTE:connectivity to LinkedIn and to be able to give out the URL easily for networking is essential for all audiences):

  • Employers
    • New Zealand based companies
      • Established technology companies that provide services to other businesses, namely website development and maintenance for other companies
      • Companies that have internalized their web development, be it internally used web based systems or companies that provide
    • Remote employers
      • Established technology companies that provide services to other businesses, namely website development and maintenance for other companies
      • Companies that have internalized their web development, be it internally used web based systems or companies that provide
  • Freelance Work
    • Work given out by companies via contract
    • This work is more likely to be received through contacts at companies

Goal

The goal is to find a way to make income post an overseas move while breaking into a new industry. Additionally, it will serve as a platform to exhibit a growing portfolio of work to lay the foundation for creating a freelance and website development business.

Persona

Demographics

Name: Billy Thorpe

Age: 47

Occupation: Billy is a recruiter at Xsolla focussing on recruiting staff for the growing Pacific Rim market.
  • Xsolla is a web development company that specifically targets gaming companies.
  • While they provide a web building tool that allows for developers to create a website for free, there are substantial services that are provided to game development studios that require some technical skill at working with their fullstack of web applications for both custom development and maintenance.
  • These roles also require soft skills since there is substantial interaction between the technical staff and the client development studio and within the team and company.

Technology Used

  • Billy uses the following technology for work:
    • Company issued MacBook Pro
    • Company issued iPhone
    • Company issued desktop for when in office (infrequent)
  • Billy owns and uses for his own benefit the following technology:
    • High-end Windows based gaming PC
    • Various consoles
    • Android tablet

Proficiency

  • Billy is highly proficient with technology
  • Billy prefers Windows and Android based tech but tolerates using Apple for work

Attitudes

  • Billy loves technology and gaming
  • Billy likes people and talking shop with other technophiles
  • Billy has a lot of work stress and needs to find talent constantly
  • Billy has had many applicants to sort through, many of whom are not qualified
  • Billy's company has had issues with hiring individuals who cannot perform; so they are shifting to offering contract to hire positions.
    • This is both good and bad - the good is that they don't have to invest a lot into the development of the talent upfront and instead the contractor has incentive to prove their worth.
    • The bad is that some contractors simply want the short gig and have no interest in becoming full-time employees and some contractors have not completed work on time and/or meet quality standards
  • Billy finds this new approach even more challenging and has to focus more on outreach than sorting through applications, although he still has to do this also.

Behaviors

  • Billy does not like being in his office and prefers to work from home or a coffee shop, which is allowed post pandemic
  • Billy has developed a quick method for screening applications - if there is no mention of some technical training or a portfolio of work, the applicant goes to the bottom of the list. This takes him 30 seconds or less in reviewing an application.
  • Billy scours LinkedIn looking for any technologically minded individuals who may be looking for work. This can be more time-consuming and Billy relies heavily on endorsements of skill to assist.
  • Billy will also review resumes on Indeed, again looking for some level of technical acumen. Like the applications Billy reviews through their in-house HR system, this review takes no more than 30 seconds perform to determine if this is an applicant worth looking at any further.
  • Billy, like many recruiters throughout the world, has been having a difficult time finding acceptable candidates, making his job much more stressful and difficult.

Frustrations

  • Billy is very frustrated with the lack of good applicants coming in through the traditional channels
  • Billy recognizes with the new labor market, post-pandemic, that there are far more entry level jobs than qualified applicants, but this is still a constant source of frustration
  • Billy's job performance is dependent on finding and obtaining good employees but with so few candidates and with increased competition for those candidates his level of effort is increased immensely

Use Case

Goals for using the site

  • Billy is scouring LinkedIn for potential new development talent and finds a link to this site
  • Billy wants to be able to quickly determine if this person:
    • has technical acumen as evidenced by a portfolio, certifications or endorsements
    • is able to produce to a quality standard as evidenced in a portfolio of work
    • is willing to be a contract to hire employee as evidenced by work history
    • is available to work with other team members in the Pacific Rim
  • Be able to contact this person if he finds evidence that they may be a quality candidate
