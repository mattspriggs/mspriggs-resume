Content Strategy

This will be a one-page website that will provide key information that can be quickly scanned by employers and companies interested in potentially:

  • Interviewing me for an open position
  • Contracting with me for freelance work
  • Offering a contract to hire position

Secondarily the page will provide increased visibility and network connections within the web development sector.

Because this site will tend to be heavy on words, the site will provide emphasis on the portfolio; emphasizing more visual and interactive content. Also, the verbiage used will be succinct and well organized, emphasizing the use of lists and background scrolling where appropriate and short, declarative sentences.

Welcome section

Navbar, images and title

  • Desktop view
    • The top navbar will be overlaid on a landscape oriented background image and will use semi-transparent buttons to quickly move to sections of the page.
    • At the lower left corner of the background image will be a headshot that will straddle the content area and be inset slightly from the left edge of the background photo.
    • My name will be slightly indented from the headshot and overlay the background image
    • Under my name will be two lines of text - Full Stack Web Developer; Resume & Portfolio - which will be wholey contained in the content area below the background image.
    • Adjacent to these lines of text and center will be an envelope icon that when clicked will open a contact modal window with the ability to email me or go to my LinkedIn profile
  • Mobile view
    • When viewed on a mobile device, the navbar will collapse to an overlaid hamburger menu in the upper right and maintain the same background image.
    • The headshot will shift to the left edge of the background image
    • The name, title and envelope icon for launching the contact modal will remain the same as the desktop view

  • Mobile view

Portfolio

The portfolio section will provide six examples of work. The work will be presented in clickable thumbnail images linking to the project depicted in the thumbnail. Each thumbnail will have a semi-transparent label with the title of the example of work. When clicked the location will open in a new tab so that the user can easily move between the main page and the location of the work.

Below the thumbnail will be a link to the GitHub repository for the code of the featured project. These links will also open in a new tab.

  • Desktop view
    • The thumbnails are arranged in two rows of three evenly spaced with middle centered on the page.
    • Immediately below the thumbnail will be a link to the GitHub repository centered on the above thumbnail.
  • Mobile view
    • The thumbnails will move to display as three rows of two, evenly spaced with the GitHub link just below each thumbnail.
